History

College Basketball Rivalries have been existed since the early 1900s. It introduces the history and evolution of intense rivalry between any two college/university teams. College Basketball Rivalries indicate that college/university teams and fans describe the "rivalry" a passion and respect.

History Question: What is the difference between "passion" and "respect" in terms of rivalry?

Passion Rivalry:

It's a type of rivalry term that describes that college/university teams and fans can hate one or more teams with a strong emotion and enthusiasm.

Respect Rivalry:

It's a type of rivarly term that describes that college/university teams and fans can show respect and a bragging rights toward competitive teams.

Examples of Passion Rivalry:

  • Kansas Vs. Missouri
  • Cincinnati Vs. Xavier
  • Arizona Vs. Arizona State
  • South Dakota Vs. South Dakota State

Examples of Respect Rivalry:

  • Duke Vs. North Carolina
  • Indiana Vs. Purdue
  • Michigan Vs. Michigan State
  • Arizona Vs. UCLA

Duke Vs. North Carolina Duke Vs. North Carolina Basketball is considered the greatest rivalry in all sports. It dates back to the early 1920s. The rivalry didn't heated up until the early 1960s when Art Heyman, a former Duke player, and Larry Brown, a former North Carolina player, brawl to each other during the game at Cameron Indoor Stadium. Since then, North Carolina and Duke have been the powerful teams in the ACC, which has combined of 11 NCCA Championships, 60 ACC Tournament Championships, and more than 100 All-Stars Players. Duke and North Carolina hate each other and are biggest rival. However, North Carolina fans who grow up in the 1970s and 1980s, tend to hate North Carolina State more than Duke because NC State was very competitive and won 2 NCCA Championships. Thus, Duke was an irrelevant team (Until Coach K became the head coach.) and got dominanted by North Carolina. Duke and North Carolina have been a mutual respect each other since the early 1990s. Although they battle to each other with top rank matchups and great players.
Indiana Vs. Purdue Indiana Vs. Purdue Basketball is considered the top 5 rivalry in terms of rivalry rankings. It dates back to the early 1900s. During the early days, Purdue has dominated Indiana in the 1900s through the late 1930s. The rivalry has reached its peak in the 1970s through the 1990s when Bobby Knight and Gene Keady were the head coaches and battle to each other in the state of Indiana. After they retired, the rivalry has been cool down. Since then, Indiana has won 5 NCCA Championships. Thus, Purdue leads Indiana in all-time series. Indiana Vs. Purdue hate each other and are biggest rival. However, Indiana fans claim that Kentucky is their biggest rival due to Indiana's dominance over Purdue in the 2000s. Thus, Indiana fans tend to root for Purdue when they play Kentucky in the NCCA March Madness or non-conference games. Indiana and Purdue respect each other due to in-state prominence and legend coaches.
Cincinnati Vs. Xavier Cincinnati Vs. Xavier Basketball is considered a hate-filled rivalry between the teams in the state of Ohio. Thus, it's known as Crosstown Shootout. It dates back to the late 1920s when Cincinnati helps Xavier to build $350,000 Schmidt Fieldhouse. One of the most memorable and brutality Crosstown Shootout was in 2011 when Kenny Frease, a former Xavier player, and Yanch Gates, a former Cincinnati player, brawl to each other before ending the game at Cintas Center. Since then, Cincinnati has won 2 NCCA Championships and leads Xavier in all-time series. Although Xaiver has won 11 out of 16 games against Cincinnati since 2011. Cincinnati and Xavier hate each other and are biggest rival due to in-state bragging rights. Cincinnati and Xavier don't respect each other due to historical tension.
Kansas Vs. Missouri Kansas Vs. Missouri is considered a hate-filled border rivalry between the teams. It dates back to the Bleeding Kansas days when Kansas was almost became a free state and referred to as "Jayhawkers", while Missouri was a slave state and referred to as "Border Ruffians". The rivalry is about heated, pranking, and brawling between the teams and fans. The annual rivalry game ended in 2012 when Kansas beats Missouri 87-86 at Allen Fieldhouse. Since then, Kansas has won 4 NCCA Championships and leads Missouri in all-time series. Kansas and Missouri hate each other and are biggest rival due to historical roots and its geographically. Kansas and Missouri don't respect each other due to historical tension.
Michigan Vs. Michigan State Michigan Vs. Michigan State is considered a fierce rivalry between the teams in the state of Michigan. It dates back to the 1909 when Michigan State beats Michigan 24-16 in Ann Arbor during the first rivalry game. However, the rivalry didn't heated up until the 1990s when Tom Izzo became the head coach of Michigan State. Whereas, Michigan and Michigan State has been competitive to each other due to top rank matchups and national prominence. Since then, Michigan has won 1 NCCA Championship and Michigan State has won 2 NCCA Championships, which combined of 3 NCCA Championships. Thus, Michigan leads Michigan State in all-times series. Michigan and Michigan State hate each other and are biggest rival due to in-state bragging rights and Izzo era. However, Michigan fans who grow up before Izzo era, tend to hate Indiana and probably Ohio State more. Under Izzo era, Michigan fans claim that they should hate Michigan State more than Ohio State because of sports reversed. Although Michigan biggest rival is Michigan State in all sports expect football. Michigan and Michigan State respect each other due to legend coaches and national prominence.
